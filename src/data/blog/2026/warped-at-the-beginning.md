Alright, a blog has gotta start somewhere, pop the cork, that kind of thing, so here we go.

In the olden times, before agentic coding was a thing, you know, about a year and a half ago, when we had to copy and paste our fingers raw if we wanted AI help, I switched to Warp terminal. The idea of just telling Warp what command I wanted and having it give it to me seemed magical.

Alas, it was a short lived affair as I soon ended up in some kind of configuration hell with Warp constantly upgrading, adding more options, messing up what was previously config, sloppily pushing their AI features all over the place and eventually I ended up in a state, where some legit commands would produce errors in Warp while working just fine in other terminals. Eventually I got tired of fighting with my terminal config and went back to good old iterm2. Turns out you do not need AI sprayed all around your terminal when you have AI running inside your terminal.

Except for one thing: being able to describe a terminal command and get it to execute was nice. It didn't work reliably and would sometimes trigger on valid commands, but when it did work…. It was nice. So after asking the Claude for some command a few times and copying it over to a running terminal like a peasant, it occurred to me that maybe we could just have a command for this? Is there some way to automatically paste the output of a command? It turns out there is!
